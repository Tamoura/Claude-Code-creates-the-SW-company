## Frequently Asked Questions

### General Questions

**Q: What is quantum computing?**

A: Quantum computing uses quantum mechanical phenomena (superposition, entanglement) to process information in ways classical computers cannot. This enables solving certain problems exponentially faster.

**Q: Do I need to understand quantum physics?**

A: No. This platform explains practical applications in plain language. Technical details are provided for those interested, but not required to understand business value.

**Q: Are any quantum use cases production-ready today?**

A: Yes. Post-quantum cryptography and certain optimization problems can be solved with current quantum computers or quantum-inspired algorithms. However, most applications are still 3-10 years from widespread use.

**Q: How do I know which use case applies to my business?**

A: Use the industry and problem type filters. Read full use case descriptions to understand if the problem matches your needs. Use the comparison tool to evaluate multiple options.

### Technical Questions

**Q: What are qubits?**

A: Qubits (quantum bits) are the basic unit of quantum information. Unlike classical bits (0 or 1), qubits can be in superposition of both states simultaneously.

**Q: What is gate depth?**

A: Gate depth measures the number of sequential quantum operations (gates) in an algorithm. Higher depth requires better error correction and longer coherence times.

**Q: What is coherence time?**

A: The time qubits can maintain their quantum state before environmental noise causes errors. Longer coherence times enable more complex algorithms.

**Q: What companies offer quantum computing?**

A: Major providers include IBM Quantum, Google Quantum AI, Amazon Braket, Microsoft Azure Quantum, IonQ, Rigetti, and D-Wave (for specific problems).

## Tips for Evaluating Use Cases

### Business Evaluation Criteria

1. **Problem Fit**
   - Does this solve a problem we have?
   - Is our current solution inadequate?
   - What's the potential value improvement?

2. **Maturity Assessment**
   - How far from production?
   - What's the risk of investing now?
   - Who else is working on this?

3. **Technical Requirements**
   - Do we have the expertise in-house?
   - Can we partner with quantum providers?
   - What's the learning curve?

4. **ROI Timeline**
   - When can we expect results?
   - What's the investment required?
   - What are the alternative approaches?

### Technical Evaluation Criteria

1. **Quantum Advantage**
   - Is there proven speedup vs. classical?
   - How significant is the advantage?
   - At what scale does advantage appear?

2. **Hardware Requirements**
   - Are required quantum computers available?
   - Can we use simulators initially?
   - What's the cost of quantum time?

3. **Algorithm Maturity**
   - Are algorithms well-developed?
   - Is there open-source code available?
   - Have others implemented successfully?
