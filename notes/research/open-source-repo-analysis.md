## RECOMMENDED NEXT STEPS

### Immediate Actions:

1. **Fork and Test** the top 5 repositories locally
2. **License Verification** - Review commercial use terms for AGPLv3 repos
3. **Market Research** - Validate vertical-specific demand
4. **Competitive Analysis** - Check existing vertical players
5. **Technical Assessment** - Evaluate customization effort

### Selection Criteria:

When choosing which repo to fork, prioritize:

1. **License Permissiveness** - MIT/Apache 2.0 > BSD > AGPL
2. **Tech Stack Match** - TypeScript/Next.js preferred
3. **Market Size** - $5B+ TAM minimum
4. **Activity Level** - Updated in last 30 days
5. **Star Count** - 1,000+ indicates validation
6. **Documentation Quality** - Can you figure it out?
7. **Customization Needs** - How much work to verticalize?

### Risk Factors:

**License Restrictions**:
- AGPL-3.0 requires source disclosure if hosted
- "Fair-code" licenses limit commercial use
- Always check if commercial licenses are available

**Technical Debt**:
- Python/Ruby/Go repos require more work for TypeScript teams
- Clojure repos (Penpot) are difficult to maintain
- Rust repos (Hyperswitch) need Rust expertise

**Market Competition**:
- CRM market is crowded (Salesforce, HubSpot)
- Analytics market has big players (Google, Mixpanel)
- Always look for underserved niches
